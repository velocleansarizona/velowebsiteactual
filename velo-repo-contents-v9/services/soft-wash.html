Surfaces We Clean with Soft Wash

Soft wash uses low-pressure water combined with safe cleaning solutions to lift dirt, algae, and mildew off surfaces that traditional high-pressure would damage.

  • Building exteriors and storefronts
  • Painted stucco surfaces (any color)

Why Choose Velo

Why Property Owners Choose Soft Wash

Not every surface can take a 3,000-PSI pressure washer. Painted stucco, tile roofs, awnings, and older exteriors get damaged by aggressive pressure — chipped paint, dislodged tiles, torn awning fabric, blown-out grout. Soft wash gets surfaces just as clean without any of that risk.

01

Safer for Painted & Delicate Surfaces

Soft wash uses pressure similar to a garden hose — 60 to 80 PSI — combined with biodegradable cleaning solutions that do the work chemically instead of mechanically. Your paint stays put, your finishes stay intact.

02

Actually Kills Algae & Mildew

High-pressure washing removes surface dirt but often leaves algae, mildew, and mold spores that grow back within weeks. Soft wash solutions neutralize the biological growth at the root, so surfaces stay cleaner longer.

03

Extends the Life of Roofs and Exteriors

Aggressive pressure washing on tile roofs, shingles, and older stucco can shave years off the life of the surface. Soft wash cleans without erosion — protecting your property's substrate and preserving your investment.

04

Right Tool for the Right Job

We use pressure washing where pressure washing works — concrete, sidewalks, dumpster pads. And we use soft wash where soft wash is safer — stucco, roofs, painted surfaces, delicate finishes. Same team, right approach for each surface.

Common Questions

Soft Wash FAQ

Is soft wash safe for painted surfaces?

Yes — it's specifically designed for painted stucco, wood siding, painted trim, and other finished surfaces. The low pressure (~60-80 PSI, similar to a garden hose) means paint stays intact, while the cleaning solutions do the actual dirt and algae removal.

What's the difference between soft wash and pressure washing?

Pressure washing uses high-pressure water (1,500–4,000 PSI) to physically blast dirt off durable surfaces like concrete. Soft wash uses low-pressure water plus cleaning solutions that neutralize algae, mildew, and dirt chemically. Soft wash is safer for delicate surfaces; pressure washing is faster on tough concrete. We do both, and we pick the right one for each surface.

Can you soft wash a tile roof?

Yes, and it's the recommended method. Tile roofs can be damaged by high-pressure washing — dislodged tiles, cracked tiles, water forced under the tiles. Soft wash removes algae, moss, and stains without any of that risk, and it extends the life of the roof.

Are the cleaning solutions safe for landscaping and pets?

The solutions we use are biodegradable and diluted for application. We pre-wet landscaping before starting and rinse afterward to protect plants, and pets should be kept indoors during the service just as a precaution. Everything breaks down quickly and doesn't leave residue.

How long does soft wash cleaning last?

Because soft wash actually kills the algae and mildew at the source (rather than just rinsing surface dirt), results typically last 1–3 years depending on the surface, sun exposure, and property location. Pressure-washed surfaces often need re-cleaning much sooner because the biological growth wasn't neutralized.
